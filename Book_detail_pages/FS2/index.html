Extinction Level Event: Book One

K.J. Jones (Author)

A motley mash-up of war vets and grad students face a new zombie drug hitting coastal Wilmington, North Carolina. Street name Zombie – it’s worse than flakka. But the hospitals lock down the people who are bitten by the users. Why if it’s only a drug?

PhD candidate Phebe Marcelino dismisses the abounding conspiracy theories. As roommates inexplicably attack each other and vaccinated pets turn furiously rabid, she seeks a logical answer and it’s not “zombies are real.”

Former Staff Sergeant Peter Sullivan believes the zombie apocalypse is finally here, albeit he’d prefer the slow, living-dead kind – they’d make great target practice. War is what he does best. Life is what he sucks at. When things grow hairy, he orders his circle of Ranger war vet buddies and a few Marines-turned-cops to bug out of town. Best laid plans, though.

Phebe sees the wild war vets as her only hope but reaching them in a city that’s panicking could prove her mettle. You never know what you’re made of until tested.

Influenced by true-life events of 2012’s ‘bath salts zombie apocalypse.’ Contains military dark humor and smartassery. A sense of humor is required.
